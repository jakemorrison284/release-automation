# Chaos Experiment Project Plan: HighPaymentErrorRate Alert

## Objectives
- Validate the effectiveness of the new alert thresholds and durations for HighPaymentErrorRate.
- Assess the response of the on-call engineering team to alerts during simulated high payment error rates.
- Identify potential improvements to minimize alert fatigue and optimize response efficiency.

## Timeline

### Preparation Phase (Now - 2026-05-09)
- Finalize setup of the controlled environment for simulating high payment error rates.
- Confirm monitoring tools and infrastructure readiness for real-time alert tracking.
- Communicate experiment plan and objectives to all involved team members and stakeholders.
- Establish feedback channels for on-call engineers during the experiment.

### Experiment Execution (2026-05-10)
- Run the chaos experiment simulating high payment error rates in the controlled environment.
- Monitor alert triggering and response times closely throughout the experiment.
- Collect quantitative and qualitative data on alert behavior and team responses.

### Feedback Gathering (2026-05-10 to 2026-05-17)
- Engage with on-call engineers to gather feedback on alert effectiveness and any issues encountered.
- Document feedback and observations systematically for review.

### Review and Follow-Up Meeting (Around 2026-05-17)
- Convene a meeting to review experiment results and feedback.
- Discuss necessary changes to alert configurations, monitoring, or response processes.
- Define action items and next steps based on the findings.

## Responsibilities
- Setup and Monitoring: Responsible team members to ensure environment and monitoring readiness.
- Communication: Project lead to update stakeholders and coordinate feedback collection.
- Feedback Collection: On-call engineers and team leads to provide insights during and after the experiment.
- Review and Action Planning: Project lead and team to analyze results and plan improvements.

## Communication
- Regular updates via team channels during preparation and execution.
- Feedback survey or direct interviews post-experiment.
- Meeting invites and agenda shared ahead of the review meeting.
